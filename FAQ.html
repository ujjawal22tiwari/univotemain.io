In ZKP-based voting, a voter can prove their vote is valid without revealing their identity or the vote itself. This makes the voting process private, secure, and verifiable. In regular voting, a vote can sometimes be linked to the voter, while ZKPs ensure anonymity and trustless verification.

A Zero-Knowledge Proof (ZKP) is a cryptographic method that allows someone to prove they know something without revealing the actual information. For example, a person can prove they are eligible to vote without revealing their identity. ZKPs help ensure privacy, security, and trust in digital systems‚Äîespecially useful in voting, authentication, and blockchain applications.
